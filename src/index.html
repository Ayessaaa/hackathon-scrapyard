What is Climate Change?

Climate change, driven by human activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation, is a major global challenge. It causes rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and rising sea levels, threatening both the environment and human livelihoods. Immediate action to reduce emissions and transition to renewable energy is essential. As GEM, we aim to raise awareness and empower communities to take steps towards a sustainable future.

Climate Change in the Philippines

The Philippines is particularly vulnerable, with an average of 20 typhoons hitting the country annually, causing significant damage to agriculture and infrastructure. Rising sea levels in the Philippines have increased at a rate of 3.2mm per year since 1993, displacing coastal communities. Immediate action to reduce emissions, adapt to changes, and transition to renewable energy is critical to mitigating the impacts of climate change.
